Direct physiological evidence

4 papers
2015 ✓ Direct — SCR + HR + Resp

Music induces universal emotion-related psychophysiological responses: comparing Canadian listeners to Congolese Pygmies

Egermann, H., Fernando, N., Chuen, L., & McAdams, S.

Frontiers in Psychology, doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2014.01341

Key finding: Increases in spectral centroid and pitch produced increased skin conductance (SCR) in both cultural groups — the strongest cross-cultural result. Canadians also showed increased respiration rate with spectral centroid rises; heart rate did not respond to spectral centroid (only to tempo). Mechanism proposed: brainstem reflexes reacting to urgency cues — a pathway independent of cultural learning.

doi: 10.3389/fpsyg.2014.01341
2026 ✓ Direct — HRV + HR + Resp

Listeners' baseline autonomic states associated with distinct music-physiology response patterns

Soliński, M., Pope, V., Lambiase, P., & Chew, E.

European Heart Journal: Imaging Methods and Practice, doi:10.1093/ehjimp/qyag013

Key finding: Spectral centroid increases were statistically significant predictors of cardiovascular change — shortened respiratory intervals and reduced LF-HRV power, consistent with sympathetic activation. Critically, the direction of effect depended on baseline autonomic state: parasympathetic-dominant listeners showed lengthened RR intervals; sympathetically-dominant individuals showed shortened RR intervals. Effect measured directly via continuous ECG and respiration.

doi: 10.1093/ehjimp/qyag013
2012 ✓ Direct — fMRI (neural)

Large-scale brain networks emerge from dynamic processing of musical timbre, key and rhythm

Alluri, V., Toiviainen, P., Jääskeläinen, I. P., Glerean, E., Sams, M., & Brattico, E.

NeuroImage, 59, 3677–3689, doi:10.1016/j.neuroimage.2011.11.019

Key finding: The second principal component of timbre features — with highest loadings from spectral centroid, spectral roll-off, high-energy/low-energy ratio, and ZCR (perceptual "brightness") — drove activation in cerebellar and default mode network regions during naturalistic music listening (fMRI). Timbral processing recruited cognition and sensory circuits. Note: this is neural rather than peripheral ANS, but provides the brain-level mechanism linking brightness to arousal circuits.

doi: 10.1016/j.neuroimage.2011.11.019
2007 ✓ Direct — SCR + HR + Resp

Relationships between musical structure and psychophysiological measures of emotion

Gomez, P., & Danuser, B.

Emotion, 7(2), 377–387, doi:10.1037/1528-3542.7.2.377

Key finding: Tempo, accentuation, and rhythmic articulation were the primary predictors of physiological arousal (SCR, HR, respiration rate). Timbre and mode were stronger predictors of valence than arousal. Direct physiological measurement throughout. Spectral/timbral features did not independently predict SCR in multivariate regression once tempo was included — important caveat for innerdance analysis where tempo and brightness often co-vary.

doi: 10.1037/1528-3542.7.2.377

Inferential evidence — subjective arousal ratings

4 papers
2017 ~ Inferential — subjective ratings

Perception and Modeling of Affective Qualities of Musical Instrument Sounds across Pitch Registers

McAdams, S., Douglas, C., & Vempala, N. N.

Frontiers in Psychology, doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2017.00153

Key finding: Higher energy arousal and tension arousal were both associated with brighter sounds and higher spectral centroids. Positive valence correlated with lower spectral slopes (more high-frequency energy) and sharper attack envelopes. All evidence is inferential — no ANS measures. Listeners rated on 9-point analogical-categorical scales; no skin conductance or heart rate recorded.

doi: 10.3389/fpsyg.2017.00153
2018 ~ Inferential — chills ratings

Suppressing the Chills: Effects of Musical Manipulation on the Chills Response

Bannister, S., & Eerola, T.

Frontiers in Psychology, 9:2046, doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02046

Key finding + important null: Brightness correlated with chills intensity ratings (r=0.62 and r=0.49); spectral centroid even stronger (r=0.70 and r=0.61). But no significant correlation between brightness/centroid and phasic skin conductance (SCR). The subjective experience of chills tracks brightness; autonomic electrodermal response does not. Critical falsification for the brightness→SCR assumption.

doi: 10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02046
2004 ~ Inferential — continuous ratings

Modeling Perceived Emotion with Continuous Musical Features

Schubert, E.

Music Perception, 21(4), 561–585

Key finding: First model of musical emotion unfolding over time using spectral centroid alongside loudness, tempo, melodic contour, and texture. Spectral centroid and texture showed limited and inconsistent predictive power for arousal ratings compared to loudness and tempo; model explained 33–73% of variance. No physiological measurement — continuous self-report ratings only.

doi: 10.1525/mp.2004.21.4.561
2017 ~ Inferential — annotator ratings

Developing a Benchmark for Emotional Analysis of Music

Aljanaki, A., Yang, Y-H., & Soleymani, M.

PLOS ONE, doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0173392

Key finding: Spectral features — centroid, flux, roll-off, flatness — were among the most useful features for predicting arousal from crowdsourced continuous annotations. Machine learning models using spectral centroid achieved strong arousal prediction (correlation up to r≈0.88). Inferential only: ground truth = human annotators' arousal ratings, not physiological measurement.

doi: 10.1371/journal.pone.0173392

1 paper
2021 ∅ Null — no significant effect

Synchrony in the periphery: inter-subject correlation of physiological responses during live music concerts

Czepiel, A., Fink, L.K., Fink, L.T., Wald-Fuhrmann, M., Tröndle, M., & Merrill, J.

Scientific Reports, doi:10.1038/s41598-021-00492-3

Key finding: In a naturalistic live concert setting, spectral centroid (alongside key clarity) produced no significant predictive relationship with physiological synchrony — skin conductance, heart rate, or respiration — in logistic regression models. Spectral centroid differed across pieces (Contemporary > Beethoven ≈ Brahms) but this did not translate into inter-subject physiological synchrony. Suggests laboratory results may not replicate in naturalistic high-complexity listening environments.

doi: 10.1038/s41598-021-00492-3

Synthesis for innerdance analysis

Spectral centroid has replicated direct physiological evidence for driving skin conductance and respiration rate — specifically from the Egermann et al. (2015) cross-cultural study — and a 2026 HRV study (Soliński et al.) now shows direct cardiac effects modulated by baseline autonomic state. Its neural substrate is documented via fMRI (Alluri et al., 2012).

✓ Spectral centroid → skin conductance (SCR): Replicated cross-culturally (Egermann 2015). Use SCR as the primary peripheral ANS marker for spectral brightness changes.
✓ Spectral centroid → HRV / cardiac: Direct evidence (Soliński 2026). Baseline autonomic state moderates direction. Important for the innerdance resilience arc — Stage 4 (SNS peak) vs. Stage 10 (PNS rebound).
✓ Spectral centroid → respiration: Direct (Egermann 2015, Gomez & Danuser 2007). Respiration rate increases with brightness — relevant to RSA amplitude analysis.
~ Spectral centroid → heart rate: Mixed. Egermann (2015) found HR responded to tempo, not centroid. Soliński (2026) found RR interval effects. Do not rely on HR alone — use HRV frequency bands.
~ Spectral centroid → subjective chills: Strong correlations (r≈0.70, Bannister & Eerola 2018) but dissociates from phasic SCR. Subjective experience and autonomic response are not the same thing.
∅ Naturalistic settings: Laboratory→concert transfer failed (Czepiel et al., 2021). Innerdance is a non-laboratory setting — treat spectral centroid effects as probabilistic, not deterministic.

Recommended feature use in innerdance pipeline: Include spectral centroid as one acoustic predictor in the OLS model (Phase 1), alongside tempo and loudness. Treat it as a sympathetic activation signal that co-varies with stage transitions — particularly at Stage 4 (Apoptosis, expected SNS peak) where the audio typically increases in brightness. The Soliński (2026) finding that autonomic baseline moderates direction is especially relevant to a within-person design.
