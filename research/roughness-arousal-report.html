Roughness, temporal modulation & neural/ANS arousal

Search angle: roughness dissonance arousal physiological skin conductance SCR

Arnal LH, Flinker A, Kleinschmidt A, Giraud A-L, Poeppel D

Human screams occupy a privileged niche in the communication soundscape

Using fMRI on N=16 participants, showed that amplitude modulations in the 30–150 Hz roughness band elicit bilateral amygdala responses significantly larger than speech at matched loudness. The amygdala — but not primary auditory cortex — was maximally sensitive to this restricted spectrotemporal window. Reaction times to rough sounds were faster; fear ratings scaled with roughness magnitude. ANS link is inferred from amygdala engagement; no skin conductance or HRV was measured.

neural / amygdala fMRI · ANS inferred
2015 Current Biology doi:10.1016/j.cub.2015.06.043
Arnal LH, Kleinschmidt A, Spinelli L, Giraud A-L, Mégevand P

The rough sound of salience enhances aversion through neural synchronisation

Intracranial EEG (n=11 epilepsy patients) during rough vs. non-rough sound stimulation. Rough sounds (30–150 Hz AM) induced sustained synchronisation of auditory networks AND large-scale salience networks — superior temporal regions, hippocampus, insula, parahippocampal cortex, frontal cortex. Cerebro-acoustic coherence peaked in the 40–130 Hz band. Aversion ratings correlated with coherence amplitude across all regions. No SCR or HRV was measured; ANS link is inferred through limbic/insula engagement.

intracranial EEG · large-scale salience networks · ANS inferred
2019 Nature Communications doi:10.1038/s41467-019-11626-7
Gorzelańczyk EJ, Podlipniak P, Walecki P, Karpiński R, Tarnowska M

Pitch syntax violations are linked to greater skin conductance changes, relative to timbral violations

N=28 musically untrained participants. Measured electrodermal activity (nS) directly during playback of tonally correct, tonally violated, and timbre-altered melodies. Timbral changes produced SCR of 855 nS vs. 247 nS for in-key notes — a 3.5× increase. Pitch violations were stronger still (1311 nS). Establishes a direct acoustic → SCR chain for timbral change; roughness per se was not isolated but timbre shifts (piano → flute) were the manipulation. Amygdala and anterior cingulate–limbic loops proposed as mediators.

direct SCR measurement · sympathetic index
2017 Frontiers in Psychology doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2017.00586
Schaefer HE

Music-evoked emotions — current studies (review)

Narrative review documenting that dissonant chords containing irrational frequency ratios evoke roughness percepts, with measured skin conductance responses and amygdala / orbitofrontal cortex activity recorded in earlier studies (Koelsch et al., Blood & Zatorre). Crescendo manipulations linked to cardiovascular modulation. The review collates existing physiological evidence rather than presenting new measurements; the roughness → SCR chain is assembled from multiple prior studies. ZCR not discussed.

review · collated evidence
2017 Frontiers in Neuroscience doi:10.3389/fnins.2017.00600

Timbral roughness, psychoacoustics & cortical arousal

Search angle: timbral roughness cortical arousal psychoacoustics EEG ANS sympathetic

Erfanian M, Oberman T, Chait M, Kang J

Loudness and sound category: their distinct roles in shaping perceptual and physiological responses to soundscapes

N=25 healthy adults. Directly measured SCR (sympathetic index) and perceptual ratings during natural vs. mechanical soundscapes at matched levels. Mechanical sounds — characterised by high-frequency spectral content, roughness, and sharpness — were rated more aversive and eventful than nature sounds at equal loudness. SCR amplitude showed significant main effect of loudness but not sound category; SCR rise-time (speed) correlated with eventfulness. Establishes that roughness-laden mechanical sounds drive faster sympathetic engagement even when loudness is controlled. Direct measurement.

direct SCR measurement · N=25
2025 eNeuro doi:10.1523/ENEURO.0146-25.2025
Erfanian M, Mitchell AJ, Kang J, Aletta F

The psychophysiological implications of soundscape: a systematic review of empirical literature and a research agenda

Systematic review of soundscape psychophysiology literature. Key finding: acoustic environments inconsistently evoke physiological responses across studies; the acoustic→ANS link is largely unestablished in soundscape research. Mechanical sounds (higher roughness) more consistently elevated SCR vs. nature sounds, but effect sizes were inconsistent across labs. Flags HRV as underused — only one reviewed study employed it. Roughness not measured as an isolated variable; reviewed studies use categorical stimulus types.

systematic review · mixed evidence
2019 Int J Environ Res Public Health doi:10.3390/ijerph16193533
Reymore L, Beauvais-Lacasse E, Smith BK, McAdams S

Modeling noise-related timbre semantic categories of orchestral instrument sounds with audio features, pitch register, and instrument family

N-participant perceptual rating study. Three semantic categories isolated: raspy/grainy/rough, harsh/noisy, airy/breathy. Harmonic-to-noise ratio (HNR) was the strongest predictor of rough ratings (R²=0.78). Both rough and harsh categories correlated negatively with positive valence (r=−0.90 and −0.61) and positively with perceived physical exertion. No physiological measurements; the arousal link is inferred from the valence-exertion-roughness cluster. ZCR not directly featured; HNR and inharmonicity are the key acoustic correlates.

perceptual ratings · no physiology
2022 Frontiers in Psychology doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2022.796422
Roque AL, Valenti VE, Guida HL, et al.

The effects of auditory stimulation with music on heart rate variability in healthy women

N participants. Compared baroque (relaxant), heavy metal (excitatory), and white noise on HRV indices. White noise — the highest-ZCR, roughest condition — specifically decreased HF power (parasympathetic withdrawal), while music styles decreased global variability through intensity rather than spectral content. RRtri and SD2 were significantly reduced by all conditions. Suggests noise-like texture (high ZCR, broadband) selectively suppresses parasympathetic tone. Direct HRV measurement; acoustic isolation partial.

direct HRV measurement · white noise comparison
2013 Clinics (São Paulo) doi:10.6061/clinics/2013(07)12
Anikin A

Acoustic estimation of voice roughness

Validated two algorithms for estimating roughness from modulation spectra. Optimal modulation frequency range refined to 50–200 Hz (narrower than earlier 30–150 Hz assumption). Achieved r=0.75/0.69 correlation with human roughness ratings. Notes rough sounds are perceived as more aversive and links roughness to dominance signalling across species. No ANS or arousal physiology measured; the arousal pathway is cited as existing literature.

perceptual validation · no physiology
2025 Attention, Perception & Psychophysics doi:10.3758/s13414-025-03060-3

ZCR, noise texture & arousal/alertness

Search angle: zero crossing rate roughness arousal alertness music + noise spectral roughness alertness cortisol sympathetic

Vuoskoski JK, Zickfeld JH, Alluri V, Moorthigari V, Seibt B

Feeling moved by music: investigating continuous ratings and acoustic correlates

Continuous rating paradigm across four music excerpts. ZCR correlated significantly with feeling moved in the vocal excerpt (Allegri) only; the authors interpret ZCR as a proxy for vocal/voice-like texture. Spectral roughness correlated positively with feeling moved in three of four joyfully-moving excerpts. Arousal-related features (loudness, spectral flux, roughness) predicted moving responses only in joyful — not sad — music. No direct physiological measurement; self-report ratings only.

continuous self-report · no physiology
2022 PLOS ONE doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0261151
Bannister S

A vigilance explanation of musical chills? Effects of loudness and brightness manipulations

Manipulated loudness and brightness of chill-inducing excerpts. Sudden increases in loudness, brightness, and — in naturalistic analysis — roughness were correlated with chill intensity across cultural styles. Proposes a "vigilance/alerting" pathway: fast acoustic transients trigger orienting responses mediated by subcortical alarm circuits, not solely reward. Chills accompanied by skin conductance increases in prior work (cited). The roughness–arousal link is proposed/inferred; no direct roughness manipulation was the IV.

behavioral + proposed mechanism
2020 Music & Science doi:10.1177/2059204320915654

ZCR as noisiness index in emotion and arousal research — state of field

No peer-reviewed study was identified that directly measures ZCR as an independent variable and records ANS physiological output (SCR, HRV, cortisol) as the dependent variable. ZCR is routinely used as a feature in music emotion recognition (MER) models where it predicts the arousal dimension computationally, but these models use self-report labels, not physiology. The closest physiological proxy: white noise (maximum ZCR condition) in Roque et al. 2013 (above) showed selective parasympathetic withdrawal. The ZCR → ANS link is therefore inferential via noise texture → roughness → amygdala/SCR chain.

gap in literature · indirect chain only

Multiple studies cited in soundscape literature

Mechanical sounds, high-frequency spectral content, and sympathetic activation

Multiple soundscape studies (reviewed in Erfanian 2019, confirmed in Erfanian 2025) demonstrate that mechanical/industrial sounds — characterised by broadband spectral content, sharpness, and roughness — consistently elevate skin conductance relative to nature sounds at equal loudness. Road traffic noise EEG studies (PMC10301920) found statistical relationships between roughness index and EEG beta power (alerting band). A direct roughness-isolated → SCR study has not been published; the effect is robust at the category level.
